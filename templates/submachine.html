Submachine Guns

Submachine guns are compact, automatic firearms designed for rapid fire in close-quarters combat. They are commonly used by military, special forces, and law enforcement. Submachine guns are valued for their high rate of fire and mobility.
Compact Models: Lightweight and easy to handle, perfect for close-range engagements. Examples include Uzi, Heckler & Koch MP5, and MAC-10.
High-Capacity Models: Equipped with larger magazines for extended fire. Examples include PPSh-41 and Thompson Submachine Gun.
Submachine guns are versatile and effective in urban warfare and tactical operations.


MP5

A widely used submachine gun known for its reliability and precision, favored by military and law enforcement.

Uzi

A compact submachine gun with a high rate of fire, popular in urban combat and close-quarters scenarios.

P90

A futuristic-looking submachine gun with a top-mounted magazine, offering high capacity and compact design.

KRISS Vector

A modern submachine gun with a unique recoil system, designed for rapid and accurate firing.

Thompson

Known as the "Tommy Gun," a classic submachine gun popular during World War II and the prohibition era.

Scorpion EVO

A lightweight and compact submachine gun with excellent ergonomics, ideal for tactical operations.
