RPGs

RPGs (Rocket-Propelled Grenades) are shoulder-fired weapons that launch explosive projectiles. They are widely used in military operations for their versatility and ability to penetrate armored targets. They are typically categorized into two main types:
Reusable Launchers: Launchers designed for multiple uses by replacing the rocket. Examples include RPG-7 and Carl Gustaf.
Disposable Launchers: Single-use launchers designed for one-time deployment. Examples include AT4 and M72 LAW.
RPGs are valued for their effectiveness in anti-tank and anti-fortification roles and are widely used in asymmetric warfare.


RPG-7

A reusable rocket launcher known for its reliability and widespread use in various conflicts.

Carl Gustaf

A versatile, reusable launcher used for anti-tank and anti-fortification purposes.

AT4

A disposable launcher with excellent penetration capabilities, widely used by modern militaries.

M72 LAW

A lightweight, single-use launcher effective against lightly armored vehicles and fortifications.

RPG-29

A powerful, reusable launcher designed for penetrating advanced armor systems.

Panzerfaust

A compact, disposable launcher originally developed during World War II, known for its simplicity.
