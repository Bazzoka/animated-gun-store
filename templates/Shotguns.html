Shotguns

Shotguns are versatile firearms designed for firing shells containing multiple pellets or a single slug. They are widely used for hunting, sport shooting, and home defense. Shotguns are typically categorized into two main types:
Pump-Action Shotguns: Firearms that use a sliding handguard to chamber each round. Examples include Remington 870, Mossberg 500, and Winchester SXP.
Semi-Automatic Shotguns: Firearms that automatically chamber the next round after firing. Examples include Benelli M4, Beretta 1301, and Franchi Affinity.
Shotguns are valued for their stopping power, wide shot spread, and adaptability in various scenarios.


Pump-Action Shotguns

Firearms that use a sliding handguard to chamber rounds. Popular models: Remington 870, Mossberg 500.

Semi-Automatic Shotguns

Firearms that automatically chamber the next round. Popular models: Benelli M4, Beretta 1301.

Double-Barrel Shotguns

Classic shotguns with two parallel barrels. Known for reliability and power.

Over-Under Shotguns

Shotguns with vertically stacked barrels. Ideal for clay shooting and hunting.

Tactical Shotguns

Shotguns designed for law enforcement and home defense. Features include extended magazines and accessory rails.

Sawed-Off Shotguns

Compact shotguns with shortened barrels, ideal for close-quarters use.
