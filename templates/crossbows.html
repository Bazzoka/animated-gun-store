Crossbows

Crossbows are mechanical ranged weapons that use a horizontal bow mounted on a stock to shoot projectiles, called bolts. They are popular for hunting, sport shooting, and historical reenactments. Crossbows are typically categorized into two main types:
Recurve Crossbows: Crossbows with a curved bow design that provides more power and accuracy. Examples include Excalibur Matrix, TenPoint Nitro X, and Ravin R26.
Compound Crossbows: Crossbows that use a system of pulleys to reduce the draw weight while increasing power. Examples include Bear Archery X-Factor, TenPoint Viper S400, and Barnett HyperGhost 405.
Crossbows are valued for their accuracy, power, and ease of use in various scenarios.
